How to Migrate Your Wordle History to e65ce0a5-wordle

  1. First, create a Wordle Migration Tool by dragging this to your browser’s bookmark bar.
  2. Secondly, save (download) your Wordle history to file by first navigating to Wordle, then clicking the previously created Wordle Migration Tool on your browser’s bookmark bar and choosing a location to save the file.
  3. Finally, restore (upload) the previously saved Wordle history file to e65ce0a5-wordle by first navigating to e65ce0a5-wordle, then clicking the Wordle Migration Tool on your browser’s bookmark bar for a second time and selecting the saved file.
Note that your Wordle history is held in your browser’s local storage and will be lost any time the storage is cleared, either manually or automatically when a private/incognito browser session is ended. Use the Wordle Migration Tool to restore a previously saved history if your browser’s local storage is cleared. You can download your Wordle history as often as you like, but you can only restore it when your browser’s local storage has been cleared.
Full Disclosure: Unfortunately, unlike me, Wordle “in-the-wild” doesn’t keep a note of which particular daily puzzles you’ve solved beyond your last win and the length of your current winning streak. So when you migrate your “in-the-wild” history to me, any puzzles beyond your last win and current streak are regarded as unsolved and may be replayed in reverse date order by clicking on the # puzzle number found on the bottom right of my settings page.
